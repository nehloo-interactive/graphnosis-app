Connecting an AI client gives it *access* to Graphnosis. A **GRAPHNOSIS.md** file makes it actually *use* Graphnosis — consistently, without you having to ask each time.

It is a short instructions file you drop into your project, or into your AI client's standing rules. It tells the assistant to do two things on its own:

- **Recall before answering** — check Graphnosis for relevant past context first, instead of guessing.
- **Remember proactively** — save decisions, to-dos, drafts, and the open questions it raises, as they come up.

The payoff: your work, your follow-ups, and even the questions your AI asked mid-answer all end up saved and indexed in your Graphnosis memory — searchable later, from any client.

## Where to put it

- **Claude Code** — add `GRAPHNOSIS.md` to your repo and reference it from `CLAUDE.md`, or paste its contents straight in.
- **Cursor** — add its contents to your Project Rules.
- **Claude Cowork and other project-based clients** — drop `GRAPHNOSIS.md` into the project's files.
- **Anywhere else** — paste it wherever your client accepts standing instructions or system context.

## The file

Copy it or download it, then drop it in. Tune it freely — trim what you don't need, or point it at specific engrams; it is a starting point, not a contract.
